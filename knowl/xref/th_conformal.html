Theorem 1.27.6. Conformal maps.

Let \(D\) be an open subset of \(\R^2\text{,}\) and let \(f\colon D\rightarrow \R^2\) be a real differentiable map. Making our usual identification of \(\C\) with \(\R^2\text{,}\) we treat \(f\) as a complex function.
  1. For all \(z_0\in D\text{,}\) \(f\) is conformal at \(z_0\) if and only if \(f\) is analytic at \(z_0\) and \(f'(z_0)\ne 0\text{.}\)
  2. Given an open subset \(U\subseteq D\text{,}\) \(f\) is conformal on \(U\) if and only if \(f\) is analytic on \(U\) and \(f'(z)\ne 0\) for all \(z\in U\text{.}\)
  3. \(f\) is conformal and injective on \(D\) if and only if \(f\) is biholomorphic.

Proof.
