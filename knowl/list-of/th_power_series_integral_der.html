Theorem 1.17.8. Power series integration and differentiation.

Let \(f(z)=\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nz^n\) be a power series with radius of convergence \(R\text{.}\)
  1. \(f\) is continuous on \(B_R(z_0)\text{.}\)
  2. \(f\) is holomorphic on \(B_R(z_0)\) and
    \begin{equation} f'(z)=a_1+2a_2z+3a_3z^2+\cdots=\sum_{n=0}^\infty (n+1)a_{n+1}z^n\text{.}\tag{1.65} \end{equation}
    In other words, we a power series representation of \(f'\) by differentiating \(f\) term by term.
  3. For any path \(\gamma\) lying in \(B_{R}(z_0)\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation} \int_\gamma f\, dz=\sum_{n=0}^\infty \int_\gamma a_n(z-z_0)\, dz\text{.}\tag{1.66} \end{equation}

Proof.
