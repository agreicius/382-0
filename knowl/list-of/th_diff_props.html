Theorem 1.9.8. Derivative properties.

Assume \(f\) and \(g\) are differentiable at \(z_0\)
  1. Linear combination.
    For all \(c,d\in \C\text{,}\) \(cf+dg\) is differentiable at \(z_0\) and
    \begin{equation} (cf+dg)'(z_0)=cf'(z_0)+dg(z_0)\text{.}\tag{1.35} \end{equation}
  2. Product.
    \(fg\) is differentiable at \(z_0\) and
    \begin{equation} (fg)'(z_0)=f'(z_0)g(z_0)+f(z_0)g'(z_0)\tag{1.36} \end{equation}
  3. Quotient rule.
    Provided \(g(z_0)\ne 0\text{,}\) \(f/g\) is differentiable at \(z_0\) and
    \begin{equation} \left(\frac{f}{g}\right)'=\frac{f'(z_0)g(z_0)-f(z_0)g'(z_0)}{g(z_0)^2}\text{.}\tag{1.37} \end{equation}

Proof.

These statements are proved in essentially the same way as the corresponding statements from elementary calculus. We omit them here.
