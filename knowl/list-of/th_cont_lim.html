Theorem 1.7.6. Continuity and limits.

Let \(f\colon D\rightarrow \C\) be a complex function.
  1. Let \(z_0\in D\) be a limit point of \(f\text{.}\) The function \(f\) is continuous at \(z_0\) if and only if \(\lim\limits_{z\to z_0}f(z)=f(z_0)\text{.}\) In other words \(f\) is continuous at \(z_0\) if and only if the limit of \(f\) at \(z_0\) exists and is equal to \(f(z_0)\text{.}\)
  2. \(f\) is continuous if and only if
    \begin{equation*} \lim\limits_{z\to z_0}f(z)=f(z_0) \end{equation*}
    for all \(z_0\in D\) that are limit points of \(D\text{.}\)

Proof.
