Theorem 1.3.14. Fundamental theorem of algebra.

Let \(f(z)\) be a nonconstant polynomial: i.e., \(\deg f\geq 1\text{.}\)
  1. Any polynomial \(f\) of degree \(n\geq 1\) has a complex root: i.e., there is a \(w\in \C\) such that \(f(w)=0\text{.}\)
  2. As a consequence, any polynomial \(f\) of degree \(n\geq 1\) factors completely over \(\C\) as
    \begin{equation*} f(z)=c\prod_{k=0}^{n-1}(z-w_k) \end{equation*}
    for some complex numbers \(c,w_k\in \C\text{,}\) \(0\leq k\leq n-1\text{.}\)
