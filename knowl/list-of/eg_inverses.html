Example 1.1.15. Inverses and quotients.

Compute the following complex numbers. Your answer must be expressed in the form \(a+bi\text{,}\) where \(a,b\in \R\text{.}\)
  1. \(z^{-1}\text{,}\) where \(z=-2-3i\)
  2. \(\displaystyle (1+i)/(-2-3i)\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 1/i\)
  4. \(z/a\text{,}\) where \(z=c+di\) and \(a=a+0i\in \R\)
  5. \(z/(bi)\text{,}\) where \(z=c+di\) and \(b\in \R\)
Solution.
  1. Using the formula (1.4), we have
    \begin{equation*} z^{-1}=\frac{-2}{4+9}+\frac{3}{4+9}\, i=-\frac{2}{13}+\frac{3}{13}\, i\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. By definition, we have
    \begin{align*} \frac{1+i}{-2-3i} \amp=(1+i)(-2-3i)^{-1} \\ \amp =(1+i)(-2/13+(3/13)\, i) \amp (\text{by above})\\ \amp = \frac{-5}{13}+\frac{1}{13}\, i\text{.} \end{align*}
  3. Using formula (1.4), we see easily that \(1/i=i^{-1}=-i\text{.}\)
  4. It is easy to see that for a real number \(a=a+0i\text{,}\) we have \(a^{-1}=1/a\text{,}\) and hence
    \begin{equation*} z/a=\frac{1}{a}(c+di)=\frac{c}{a}+\frac{d}{a}\, i\text{.} \end{equation*}
  5. It is easy to see, in general that \((uw)^{-1}=u^{-1}w^{-1}\) for any complex numbers \(u,w\text{.}\) Thus we have
    \begin{align*} z/(bi) \amp =z(bi)^{-1}\\ \amp = z\cdot \frac{1}{b}(-i) \amp \text{(by above)}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{b}(-i z)\\ \amp = \frac{d}{b}-\frac{c}{b}\, i\text{.} \end{align*}
