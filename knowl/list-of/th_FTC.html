Theorem 1.12.10. Complex-valued functions: fundamental theorem of calculus.

Let \(f\colon [a,b]\rightarrow \C\) be continuous.
  1. The function \(F(s)=\int_a^s f(t)\, dt\) is differentiable on \([a,b]\) and satisfies \(F'(s)=f(s)\) for all \(s\in [a,b]\text{.}\)
  2. If \(G\colon [a,b]\rightarrow \C\) satisfies \(G'(t)=f(t)\) for all \(t\in [a,b]\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation*} \int_a^b f(t)\, dt=G(b)-G(a)\text{.} \end{equation*}
