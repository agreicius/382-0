Theorem 1.10.5. Cauchy-Riemann converse.

Assume \(f=u+iv\) and that \(z_0=x_0+iy_0\) is an interior point of the domain of \(f\text{.}\) If the first-order partial derivatives of \(u\) and \(v\) are continuous at \((x_0,y_0)\) and satisfy the Cauchy-Riemann equations, then \(f\) is differentiable at \(z_0\text{.}\)
