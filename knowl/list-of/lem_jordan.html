We have
\begin{equation*} \sin \theta\geq \frac{2}{\pi}\theta \end{equation*}
for all \(\theta\in [0,\pi/2]\text{.}\) As a consequence, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_0^{\pi}e^{-c\sin\theta}\, d\theta\leq \int_{0}^\pi e^{-c\theta}\, d\theta=\frac{1}{c}(1-e^{-c\pi}) \end{equation*}
for any \(c> 0\text{.}\)
