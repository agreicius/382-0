Theorem 1.3.10. Elementary properties of polynomials.

  1. If \(f\) and \(g\) are polynomials, then so are \(f+g\text{,}\) \(fg\text{,}\) and \(cf\) for any \(c\in \C\text{.}\)
  2. If \(f\) is a polynomial and \(w_0\in \C\) is a root of \(f\text{,}\) then there is a polynomial \(g\) such that
    \begin{equation} f(z)=(z-w_0)g(z)\text{.}\tag{1.15} \end{equation}
  3. If \(f\) is a polynomial of degree \(n\text{,}\) then \(f\) has at most \(n\) distinct roots.
