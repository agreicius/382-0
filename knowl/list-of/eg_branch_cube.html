Example 1.11.7. Branches of cube-root function.

Let \(U=\{z\in \C\colon \Re z\geq 0\}-\{0\}\text{.}\)
  1. Show that there are exactly three branches of \(\mathcal{F}(z)=z^{1/3}\) on \(U\) and give explicit formulas for each.
  2. Verify that (1.46) holds for one of your three branches.
Solution.
Since \(U\subseteq \C-R_{-\pi/2}\text{,}\) the function
\begin{align*} f(z)\amp =e^{\frac{1}{3}\Log_{-\pi/2}z}\\ \amp = e^{1/3(\ln\abs{z}+i\Arg_{-\pi/2} z)}\\ \amp =r^{1/3}e^{i(\Arg_{-\pi/2} z)/3} \amp (r=\abs{z}) \end{align*}
is a branch of \(z^{1/3}\text{.}\) According to Theorem 1.11.6, the other branches are of the form
\begin{equation*} g(z)=e^{2\pi i k/3}f(z)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Letting \(k\) vary in this manner, we see that the multiplying factor \(e^{2\pi i k/3}\) ranges over the three cube-roots of unity: i.e., the distinct branches of \(z^{1/3}\) on \(U\) are
\begin{equation*} f(z), \zeta f(z), \zeta^2 f(z)\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\zeta=e^{2\pi i/3}=\cos(2\pi/3)+i\sin(2\pi/3)\text{.}\) Again using, Theorem 1.11.6, we have
\begin{align*} f'(z)\amp =\frac{1}{3}\, \frac{f(z)}{z} \\ \amp =\frac{\abs{z}^{1/3}e^{i(\Arg_{-\pi/2} z)/3}}{3\abs{z}e^{i \Arg_{-\pi/2}(z)}}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3}\abs{z}^{-2/3}e^{-(2\Arg_{-\pi/2} z)i/3}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3} e^{-\frac{2}{3}\Log_{-\pi/2} z}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3} h(z)\text{,} \end{align*}
where \(h(z)=e^{-\frac{2}{3}\Log_{-\pi/2} z}\) is the \(-\pi/2\)-cut branch of \(z^{-2/3}\text{.}\) In other words, we have a derivative formula very much in the spirit of \(\frac{d}{dx}(x^{1/3})=\frac{1}{3}x^{-2/3}\text{.}\)
