Corollary 1.17.13. Analytic implies holomorphic.

Let \(U\subseteq \C\) be an open set. If \(f\) is analytic on \(U\text{,}\) then \(f\) is holomorphic on \(U\text{.}\) Furthermore, for any \(z_0\in U\) we have power series representation \(f(z)=\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n(z-z_0)^n\) with positive radius of convergence, where
\begin{equation} a_n=\frac{1}{n!}f^{(n)}(z_0)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{(z-z_0)^{n+1}}\text{,}\tag{1.68} \end{equation}
where \(\gamma\) is the counterclockwise oriented boundary of any closed disk \(\overline{B}\subseteq U\) containing \(z_0\text{.}\)
