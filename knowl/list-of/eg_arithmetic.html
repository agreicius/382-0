Example 1.1.7. Complex arithmetic.

Let \(z=-2+3i\text{,}\) \(w=4+i\text{.}\) Compute the following complex numbers. Your answer must be expressed in the form \(a+bi\text{,}\) where \(a,b\in \R\text{.}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle z+w\)
  2. \(\displaystyle z-w\)
  3. \(\displaystyle z\, w\)
  4. \(a\cdot z\) (where \(a=a+0i\) is real)
  5. \(bi\cdot z\) (where \(bi=0+bi\) is purely imaginary)
Solution.
  1. We have
    \begin{align*} z+w \amp =(-2+4)+(3+1)i\\ \amp = 2+4i\text{.} \end{align*}
  2. We have
    \begin{align*} z-w \amp = (-2-4)+(3-1)i \\ \amp = -6+2i\text{.} \end{align*}
  3. We have
    \begin{align*} z\, w \amp = (-2\cdot 4-3\cdot 1)+(-2\cdot 1+3\cdot 4)i\\ \amp = -11+10i\text{.} \end{align*}
  4. We have
    \begin{align*} a\, z \amp = (a+0i)(-2+3i)\\ \amp = (-2a-0\cdot 3)+(3a+0\cdot (-2))i\\ \amp = -2a+3a\, i\text{.} \end{align*}
  5. We have
    \begin{align*} bi\cdot w \amp = (0+bi)(4+i)\\ \amp = -b+4b\, i\text{.} \end{align*}
