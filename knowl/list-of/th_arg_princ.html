Theorem 1.24.9. Argument principle.

Let \(U\) be an elementary domain, and assume \(f\) is meromorphic on \(U\text{.}\) Let \(\gamma\) be a closed path in \(U\) that does not contain any zeros or poles of \(f\text{.}\)
  1. Argument principle.
    We have
    \begin{equation} \int_\gamma \frac{f'}{f}\, dz=\chi_{f\circ \gamma}(0)\text{.}\tag{1.92} \end{equation}
    In other words, the logarithmic derivate integral on the left side of (1.92) is equal to the winding number of the path \(f\circ\gamma\) around \(0\text{.}\)
  2. Zeros and poles theorem.
    Let \(Z\) be the set of zeros of \(f\) in \(U\text{,}\) and let \(P\) be the set of poles of \(f\) in \(U\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation} \int_\gamma \frac{f'}{f}\, dz=\sum_{z\in Z}\chi_\gamma(z)\ord_f z+\sum_{z\in P}\chi_\gamma(z)\ord_f z\text{.}\tag{1.93} \end{equation}
    In particular, if \(\chi_\gamma(z)=1\) for all \(z\in Z\cup P\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation} \int_\gamma \frac{f'}{f}\, dz=\sum_{z\in Z}\ord_f z+\sum_{z\in P}\ord_f z.\tag{1.94} \end{equation}
    In this special case, we say summarize (1.94) as
    \begin{equation*} \int_\gamma \frac{f'}{f}\, dz=\#(\text{zeros of } f)-\#(\text{poles of } f)\text{,} \end{equation*}
    where our count “includes multiplicities”: i.e., we take into account the order of each zero and pole of \(f\text{.}\)

Proof.
