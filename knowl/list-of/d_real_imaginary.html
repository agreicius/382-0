Definition 1.1.4. Real and imaginary numbers.

Let \(z=a+bi\) be a complex number. We say that \(z\) is real if \(b=\Im z=0\text{,}\) and (purely) imaginary if \(a=\Re z=0\text{.}\) Henceforth we identify the real numbers \(\R\) with the real complex numbers: i.e., we declare that
\begin{equation*} \R=\{z\in \C\colon \Im z=0\}=\{a+0i\colon a\in \R\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Furthermore we will use the following abbreviations for various real and purely imaginary complex numbers:
\begin{align*} a+0i \amp =a \amp 0+bi\amp =bi\\ a+(-b)i \amp =a-bi \amp a+(\pm 1)\,i \amp = a\pm i\text{.} \end{align*}
