Example 1.27.7. Conformal map: squaring function.

Let \(f(z)=z^2\text{,}\)
  1. Show that \(f\) is conformal on \(\C-\{0\}\)
  2. Illustrate with an explicit geometric example that \(f\) does not preserve the angle between curves intersecting at \(z_0=0\text{.}\)
Solution.
  1. Clearly \(f\) is analytic everywhere, and \(f'(z)=2z\) is nonzero at all \(z\ne 0\text{.}\) Thus \(f\) is conformal on \(\C-\{0\}\) by TheoremÂ 1.27.6.
  2. Consider the rays \(R_0\) and \(R_{\pi/4}\text{,}\) which intersect at \(z_0=0\) at an angle of \(\pi/4\text{.}\) However, their images under \(f\) are \(f(R_0)=R_0\) and \(f(R_{\pi/4})=R_{\pi/2}\text{,}\) which intersect at an angle of \(\pi/2\text{.}\)
