Theorem 1.8.10. Continuity properties.

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be complex functions.
  1. Write \(f=u+iv\) in terms of its real and imaginary parts. We have \(f\) is continuous at \(x_0\) if and only if \(u\) and \(v\) are continuous at \(x_0\text{.}\)
  2. If \(f\) and \(g\) are continuous at \(z_0\text{,}\) then so are
    \begin{align*} \overline{f} \amp \amp \abs{f}\amp \amp cf \ (\text{for any } c\in \C) \\ f+g \amp \amp fg \amp \amp f/g \ (\text{provided } g(z_0)\ne 0) \text{.} \end{align*}
  3. If \(g\) is continuous at \(x_0\) and \(f\) is continuous at \(g(x_0)\text{,}\) then \(f\circ g\) is continuous at \(x_0\text{.}\)
