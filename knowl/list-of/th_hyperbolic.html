Theorem 1.7.13. Complex hyperbolic functions.

  1. For all \(z\in \C\) we have
    \begin{align*} \cos(iz) \amp = \cosh(z) \amp \sin(iz)\amp= i\sinh(z)\text{.} \end{align*}
  2. For all \(z=x+iy\in \C\) we have
    \begin{align*} \cos(z) \amp =\cos(x)\cosh(y)-i\sin(x)\sinh(y)\\ \sin(z) \amp =\sin(x)\cosh(y)+i\cos(x)\sinh(y)\text{.} \end{align*}

Proof.

  1. These identities follow directly from Definition 1.7.10 and Definition 1.7.12.
  2. These identities follow directly from the sum identities in (1.29) and Definition 1.7.12.
