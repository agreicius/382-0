Theorem 1.25.3. Inverse function theorem.

Assume \(f\) is analytic at \(z_0\) and that \(f'(z_0)\ne 0\text{.}\) There is an open ball \(B=B_r(z_0)\) satisfying the following properties.
  1. \(f\) is injective on \(B\) and \(U=f(B)\) is open.
  2. The inverse map \(f^{-1}\colon U\rightarrow B\) is analytic and satisfies
    \begin{align*} f^{-1}(w) \amp =\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{\gamma}\frac{zf(z)}{f(z)-w}\, dz\, \gamma(t)=z_0+re^{it}, t\in [0,2\pi]\\ (f^{-1})'(w) \amp = \frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(w))} \end{align*}
    for all \(w\in U\text{.}\)

Proof.
